Rules Box — 17 Design Vibes

17 ways to present the three kinds of procedural info together: procedure Rules (bylaws), Alerts (triggered rules from the transcript), and Cards (Robert's Rules deck). Some designs keep them split, others blend them. Mini tags show which approach each uses.

● Rule — bylaws / procedure ● Alert — transcript-triggered ● Card — Robert's Rules deck

Book of Rules

Split
An open book. Left page = rules, right page = cards. Flip section tabs to see alerts. Ceremonial, parliament-library vibe.
Part XI

Motions

A motion must be moved and seconded before being debated. A motion to amend must be relevant and only one amendment to an amendment is allowed at a time.

Voting proceeds as set out in Part XII; every member present shall vote unless recused.

Card IV

Amend

Changes the main motion by adding, striking, or substituting words. Subsidiary motion; requires a second and majority vote.

"I move to amend the motion by [insert change]."
